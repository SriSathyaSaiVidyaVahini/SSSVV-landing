Mission English

What is Mission English?

In aspirational India, learning English is considered a crucial component in the pursuit of social mobility and gainful employment. English has come to represent people’s aspirations, and proficiency in the language helps them to participate fully in national and international life. People view English as a unifying force in a global world. Language learners look forward to the felicity of expressing themselves fluently in a language, which opens avenues of success in life. English helps bind people from different states together, especially in a heterogeneous environment like India. As we move towards progress in an increasingly interconnected world, we are witnessing a notable increase in migration, both within our country and to foreign nations. English is, therefore, an essential skill for further education and employment in the country today.

Sri Sathya Sai Vidya Vahini offers programmes to help teachers and the youth of the country to empower themselves in English language proficiency through its Mission English programmes.

Our Offerings

1. The Phone Your Friend (PYF) Programme

A volunteer-driven initiative that has come as a boon to adult learners, a PYF volunteer mentors a teacher over the phone for a one-hour English immersion class that is divided into three sessions in a week depending on the mutual convenience of both the teacher and the PYF volunteer. Leveraging technology, the lessons are provided by trainers through WhatsApp calls to the teachers of select schools in India. 176 learners are currently benefiting through the PYF.

2. Sain In–The English App

To expand its reach and impact, SSSVV has developed Sain In – The English App, aimed at introducing the basics of the English language to a larger number of aspirants across the country. This mobile application is thoughtfully designed to combine human interaction with automated learning, enabling mentors to guide a greater number of learners effectively. It can benefit more applicants as the app enables mentors to take more learners under their wing. The app allows users to learn English at their own pace, free of cost, and is especially tailored for speakers of vernacular languages. Similar to the PYF Programme, the app focuses on all four core language skills—listening, speaking, reading, and writing—through dedicated segments such as Listen Keenly, Speak Well, Read Aloud, and Write Right. Currently, 1,074 learners are registered on the app. Teachers from over 100 schools across India are enrolled in this app-based English learning initiative. Nearly 500 mentors are volunteering their time to support and guide these interested learners.

3. The English Immersion Programme

This five-module course focuses on developing essential English language skills for students of Classes 9 to 12, aiming to prepare them for the English component of entrance examinations for higher education and enhance their performance in school-level English assessments. A total of 4500 students from across India are enrolled in the programme which is available on SSSVV’s learning management system student.srisathyasaividyavahini.org (EIP).

4. Happy Hour English Classes (HHEC)

A focused, once-a-week intervention for students of Classes 3 to 5 aimed at improving their spoken English skills, the Happy Hour English Class will gradually be made available to all students up to Grade 8 in the coming years.
